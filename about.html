Why It Matters Who You Work With

You can find qualified practitioners anywhere. Qualifications matter — but they're not what transforms you.

Your nervous system learns from the nervous system working with yours. Neuroscience calls it co-regulation — when the person guiding you has done the deep work themselves, your system starts to calibrate to theirs. Not because they told you to relax. Because their body is teaching yours what regulated actually feels like.

That's why who you work with isn't a detail. It's the thing that makes or breaks the entire process.

The person guiding your shift should embody it. Not perform it. Not theorise about it. Actually live it.

The Science
Mirror neurons fire when you're in the presence of someone who has genuinely transformed. Your nervous system doesn't just hear the words — it feels the regulation.

My Story

I started my own therapeutic work at fifteen.

At fifteen, I left home for boarding school in Scotland — a different country, no family, no safety net. Within months, I developed a severe eating disorder. Three years of it. The kind where you don't know if you're going to come out the other side.

My godmother — a hypnotherapist and psychologist specialising in psychosomatics — was the first person who showed me what real therapeutic work could do. Not just talking about the problem. Reaching the part of me that was running it. That was the moment I understood: the mind can change the body. The subconscious can be rewritten. And the right person guiding you changes everything.

Through that work, I learned to reconnect with myself — my body, my impulses, my voice. To actually hear myself instead of overriding every signal. To be on my own team instead of at war with myself.

I healed the eating disorder completely. I've been free of it for eleven years.
