The Inner Shift Method

Three clinical modalities — cognitive behavioural hypnotherapy, rapid transformational therapy, and somatic release work — integrated into one system. Not a menu. A method designed to reach the pattern everywhere it lives.

Five phases. Each one builds on the last.

Notice

Phase 1 — Seeing what's been running on autopilot
What happens

We map your patterns. Not just what you do, but the belief system underneath it — the rules, the assumptions, the “I have to” and “I should” and “I'm not allowed to” that run quietly in the background of every decision you make.

This phase draws on cognitive behavioural frameworks to identify the specific thoughts, beliefs, and behavioural loops that are keeping the pattern in place.

What it feels like
Like seeing the matrix. Clients often say, “I never realised I was doing that.” Most of these patterns are so deeply embedded they feel like personality. They're not. They're programming.

Feel

Phase 2 — Reconnecting with the body's record
What happens

We go underneath the story. Before we can release anything, we need to reconnect with what the body is actually holding. Most people are so disconnected from physical sensation that they don't realise they've been bracing for years.

We slow down. We listen inward. Not releasing yet — just noticing what's there. You can't let go of something you haven't fully acknowledged.

What it feels like
Uncomfortable, sometimes. Revealing, always. Many clients are surprised to discover they've been numb to sensations they assumed were normal — the tight chest, the held breath, the tension they carry as baseline.

Release

Phase 3 — Letting the body let go
What happens

This is where the body lets go of what it's been holding. Using somatic techniques, we work with your nervous system to discharge the trapped survival energy — the fight-or-flight responses that got frozen in place because they had nowhere to go at the time.

We go slowly — approaching the activation in small, manageable doses, moving between the difficult sensation and safety, so your nervous system learns it can let go without falling apart.

What it feels like
Different for everyone. Some clients feel physical warmth, shaking, deep breaths, or a sudden wave of emotion. Some feel lighter. Some feel nothing dramatic — just a quiet “something shifted.” There's no right way to release.

Rewire

Phase 4 — Changing the programme at its core
What happens

This is where we change the meaning. The old beliefs — “I'm not enough,” “I have to earn love,” “it's not safe to be seen” — get identified, challenged, and replaced. Through cognitive restructuring and hypnosis, we rewrite the core beliefs that have been running your identity, your decisions, and your emotional reactions.

Sometimes that involves regression — going back to the moment a belief was first formed and reprocessing it from your adult perspective. Sometimes it's done through direct cognitive work: disputing the old rule, building a new one, and installing it under hypnosis where it can reach the subconscious. The tool depends on the client. The result is the same: the old programme loses its authority. A new one takes its place.

What it feels like
Like something clicks into place. Not just intellectually — you feel it shift. The belief that ran everything quietly in the background suddenly looks like what it is: a decision a child made to survive. And you get to make a different one.

Become

Phase 5 — Living from the new wiring
What happens

Integration. This is where you learn to maintain the shift. We build practical scaffolding — self-hypnosis you can use on your own, thought restructuring skills, coping strategies for when old patterns resurface — so the rewiring holds.

The goal of CBH is to make you your own therapist. Not to create dependency on sessions, but to equip you with the tools to catch old patterns when they resurface and redirect them yourself.

What it feels like
Like growing into someone you already were underneath. Not performing a new identity — shedding the one you built to survive. The old triggers still exist — but they've lost their charge.
The goal isn't to become someone new. It's to stop betraying the person you already are.

01

Cognitive Behavioural Hypnotherapy (CBH)

The structure. The resilience. The tools you keep.

Most people hear “hypnotherapy” and picture someone swinging a watch. CBH is nothing like that. It's an evidence-based clinical method that combines the structured framework of cognitive behavioural therapy with the focused attention state of hypnosis — and the research behind it is substantial.

What makes CBH different from traditional hypnotherapy is the framework. It uses structured cognitive restructuring — identifying the beliefs that drive your patterns, disputing them with precision, and replacing them with new ones. Then hypnosis installs those new beliefs at the subconscious level, where they can actually take hold. Add imagery rehearsal (mentally rehearsing new responses until they feel natural) and self-hypnosis training (so you can reinforce the work between sessions), and you have a method designed to make you your own therapist — not dependent on one.

I hold a Level 5 Practitioner Diploma in Cognitive Behavioural Hypnotherapy — a 500-hour clinical training programme from the UK College of Hypnosis & Hypnotherapy, externally verified by NCFE, one of the UK's national awarding bodies.

Role in the method
CBH is the backbone. It provides the cognitive mapping in Notice, the belief restructuring in Rewire, and the practical skills in Become — it runs through the entire method.
02

Rapid Transformational Therapy (RTT)

The deep access. The root cause. The rewrite.

Developed by Marisa Peer, RTT combines principles from hypnotherapy, psychotherapy, and cognitive behavioural techniques to access and transform the root cause of an issue — not just manage symptoms.

The core mechanism is regression under hypnosis. You're guided back to the moments where a limiting belief was first formed — not to relive them, but to reprocess them from your adult perspective. RTT works directly with the subconscious mind, accessing the programming at the level where it was installed.

After the session, you receive a personalised recording to reinforce the new beliefs over the following weeks — the difference between having a breakthrough and maintaining it.

Role in the method
RTT contributes to the Rewire phase — particularly when regression to root cause moments is the right tool for the client's situation.
03

Somatic Release Work

The body's record. The nervous system reset.

The body stores what the mind can't process. When a survival response — fight, flight, or freeze — doesn't complete, the energy stays trapped. This is why you can understand a pattern intellectually and still feel the anxiety in your chest.

Rooted in the work of trauma researcher Peter Levine, somatic techniques focus on completing those interrupted responses — not through re-telling the story, but through the body itself. Your nervous system learns it can face what it's been avoiding and return to calm.

Role in the method
Somatic work is central to the Feel and Release phases — locating what the body is holding and giving it a way to finally let go.
Why The Full Integration

The belief lives in your thinking. The original installation lives in your subconscious. The charge lives in your body.

If you only address one, the others keep the pattern alive.

The value isn't in having three modalities — it's in the clinical judgment to know which tool to reach for in any given moment, and how to sequence them so they reinforce each other.
